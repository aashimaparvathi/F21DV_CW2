ROOTS OF THE COFFEE CULTURE

The influence of location, and social & environmental factors.

The map shows the geographical positions of the top 25 coffee-consuming
countries, along with their per capita and total coffee consumption.


The chart explores three potential reasons for high coffee consumption:

- higher leisure hours (min per day of people between 15-64)
- lower land temperatures (in celsius)
- lower annual sunshine duration (hours in winter annually)

THE IMPACT OF THE ADDICTION

Positive Outlook: The charts indicate that the top coffee consuming countries
rank higher in terms of life-satisfaction & work productivity.

Negative Outlook: Coffee is in the top 6 of products causing environmental
degradation in terms of GHG emission & agricultural land use.


Of 33 food products considered, Coffee is the third largest producer of Greenhouse gases, and takes sixth place based on its land footprint.
